Ayimolou

Ingredients

  • 2 cups white beans (or “haricots” as used in the tradition).
  • 1 large onion (chopped).
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or red palm oil).
  • 1–2 whole hot peppers (e.g., Scotch bonnet or local variety).
  • Salt to taste.
  • 1 teaspoon smoked or dried shrimp powder (optional).
  • 1 cup chopped tomato or tomato sauce (optional).
  • Gari (cassava flour) or plantains or bread, for accompaniment.

Cooking Steps

  1. Rinse the beans and soak if desired, then drain.

  2. Place the beans in a pot, cover with water, add salt and bring to a boil until tender.

  3. In a separate pan heat the oil until hot.

  4. Add chopped onion and garlic to the oil and sauté until fragrant and translucent.

  5. Add the hot pepper whole (or chopped if preferred) and stir for a minute.

  6. If using, stir in tomato or tomato sauce and cook briefly until just heated through.

  7. Add the cooked beans into the sauté mixture, stir to combine.

  8. Season with black pepper, shrimp powder (if using) and adjust salt.

  9. Reduce heat, cover and simmer for a few minutes so flavours meld and sauce thickens slightly.

  10. Remove from heat, discard whole pepper if desired, and stir once more before serving.

Serving steps

  1. Spoon the Vehi into a bowl or plate, positioning it as the base of the meal.

  2. Serve with your chosen accompaniment (gari, plantains, bread or rice) alongside or underneath.

  3. Optionally garnish with sliced onions or a drizzle of extra oil, and serve hot for best flavour.

  4. Add Mgbagba(Tomato stew) and Yebesse Fionfion(Spicy sauce).

  5. Enjoy!
