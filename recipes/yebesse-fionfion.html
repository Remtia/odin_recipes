Yebesse Fionfion

Ingredients

  • Dried red chili peppers (or fresh hot chili), main heat source.
  • Onions, chopped.
  • Garlic and/or ginger, minced or grated.
  • Smoked fish or dried fish (or shrimp powder) for umami.
  • Tomato paste or fresh tomatoes (optional, some versions include tomato).
  • Vegetable oil (or palm oil) to fry the mixture.
  • Salt, ground black pepper, and seasoning cube (optional) for taste.
  • Optional spices: anise powder, fenugreek, or local indigenous spices.

Cooking Steps

  1. Chop the onions, garlic, ginger and pulses of chili peppers.

  2. Heat vegetable (or palm) oil in a heavy saucepan over medium heat.

  3. Add the chopped onions, garlic and ginger, sauté until fragrant and translucent.

  4. Add the chopped or whole chili peppers and stir for a minute to release their heat.

  5. If using, stir in tomato paste (or fresh tomatoes) and fry until the oil begins to separate.

  6. Add the smoked fish (or dried fish) or shrimp powder, and stir to combine thoroughly.

  7. Season with salt, ground black pepper, and any optional spices or seasoning cube.

  8. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the mixture, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the oil rises to the top.

  9. Taste and adjust the heat/seasoning to your preference, then remove any whole chili pods if used.

  10. Let the sauce cool slightly and transfer to a clean jar or container; it’s now ready.

Serving steps

  1. Spoon the Yebesse Fionfion sauce into a small bowl or ramekin as a condiment.

  2. Spoon the Yebesse Fionfion sauce into a small bowl or ramekin as a condiment.

  3. Add it to the dish of your choice, Ayimolou or Jollof Rice.

  4. Enjoy!
