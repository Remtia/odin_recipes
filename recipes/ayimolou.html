Ayimolou

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice (local white rice preferred).
  • 1 cup red beans (haricots rouges) or niébé beans.
  • 1 large onion, chopped.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional).
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or palm-oil for deeper flavour).
  • 1-2 hot peppers (whole or chopped) for heat.
  • Salt to taste.
  • Black pepper to taste.
  • Optional: smoked or dried shrimp powder or small piece of smoked fish/ meat for extra flavour.

Cooking Steps

  1. Rinse the beans and soak for 30 mins (if needed), then drain and set aside.

  2. In a large pot, add the beans and enough water, bring to a boil and cook until beans are tender but still hold shape.

  3. Meanwhile, rinse the rice and set aside.

  4. In another pot, heat the oil over medium heat and sauté the chopped onion (and garlic if using) until translucent.

  5. Add the hot pepper (whole or chopped) and stir for a minute to release flavour./p>

  6. Add the cooked beans (with a little of their cooking water) into the onion-pepper mixture and stir to combine.

  7. Add the rice on top of the bean mixture and pour in enough water to cook the rice (ratio around 1:2 rice:water, adjust as needed).

  8. Season with salt and black pepper (and optional shrimp/fish flavouring) and stir gently once to distribute.

  9. Cover the pot, reduce heat to low, and cook until the rice has absorbed the water and is tender (about 15-20 mins).

  10. Once done, fluff gently with a fork, discard the whole pepper if used, and set aside off-heat for a few minutes to settle.

Serving steps

  1. Spoon the Ayimolou onto a plate or into a bowl, creating a neat mound or portion.

  2. Style it by adding spaghetti and fresh onion.

  3. Add cooked eggs.

  4. Add Mgbagba(Tomato stew) and Yebesse Fionfion(Spicy sauce).

  5. Add fried fish(optional).

  6. Enjoy!
