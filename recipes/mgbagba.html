Mgbagba

Ingredients

  • 10 large ripe tomatoes (or equivalent canned tomato).
  • 2 large onions.
  • 2 cloves of garlic (or 2 tbsp ground garlic).
  • 3 tbsp fresh grated ginger.
  • 3 tbsp dried shrimp powder (or shrimp seasoning).
  • 1-3 West Indian peppers (scotch bonnet/habanero) or 5 “gboyébssé” peppers.
  • 4 tsp tomato paste.
  • Salt & ground black pepper to taste.
  • “Potash” (a small pea-sized amount) used traditionally for thickening/colour adjustment.
  • 1 seasoning cube (optional).
  • 1 cup vegetable oil (or enough to fry).

Cooking Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.

  2. Add chopped onions and sauté for ~2 minutes until translucent.

  3. Stir in the tomato paste and fry it until it darkens slightly and the oil begins to separate.

  4. Add the chopped tomatoes (or canned tomatoes) and cook for ~5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. Mix in the garlic, ginger, dried shrimp powder, chopped pepper(s), salt, black pepper, and seasoning cube.

  6. Sprinkle in the small amount of potash, stir well (this helps with color and texture).

  7. Reduce heat to low, cover and let the stew simmer slowly until the onions are fully tender and the sauce has thickened about 10 minutes or more.

  8. Adjust seasoning (taste for salt, pepper, heat) and continue to simmer until the oil floats on top and the sauce has reached a deep rich red color.

  9. Remove from heat, giving it a final stir to integrate the flavours and ensure nothing is sticking to the pot.

  10. Let it rest for a minute, then it’s ready to accompany your main dish.

Serving steps

  1. You can place the Mgbagba stew in a serving bowl or ladle it over a starch (e.g., rice, yam, plantain) of your choice.

  2. Arrange your main protein (fried chicken, fish or beef) alongside or on top of the stew for a complete plate.

  3. Add it to the dish of your choice, Ayimolou or Jollof Rice.

  4. Enjoy!
